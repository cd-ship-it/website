letterParagraphs:
  - en: |
      The Board of Trustees (BOT) has identified the need to openly address and respond to a few events that have occurred within Crosspoint Church (“the Church”) and its congregation over the last several months. One concern surrounds former and departing pastors registering to establish new churches unaffiliated with Crosspoint Church in close proximity, while retaining and actively serving in a ministry role within the Church. A similar concern is founded in several members of the congregation who are serving in various leadership positions and capacities (registered agents or officers) at another church but have continued to retain their membership with Crosspoint Church. While Crosspoint Church appreciates the heart and dedication of its members to serve, the BOT foresees these actions, among others, may result in possible conflicts of interest and immediate impacts adverse to the forward progress of the Church.
  - en: |
      As such, the BOT, in conjunction with its advisory council, strongly believe that it is imperative for Crosspoint Church to designate a distinct leader, even in the short-term, to manage expectations and guide the Church through these unexpected, and frankly uncomfortable, circumstances. It is the responsibility of the BOT to act promptly to address the situation at hand and preserve the Church’s desire to function as a disciple-making church to the fullest extent.
  - en: |
      Having discussed this priority extensively and acknowledging the weight of this decision, along with the endorsement of the majority of campus pastors, we appoint Pastor Abraham Chiu as the Interim Senior Pastor (ISP). This position is subject to renewal on an annual basis, up to a maximum of three (3) years. The ISP will not be considered as a candidate for the Senior Pastor position. We understand this may lead to further questions, so we ask for your patience and understanding throughout this transition. Pastor Chiu will share his priorities, responsibilities, and goals as ISP with the congregation over the next few weeks. In doing so, the BOT will be able to refocus its efforts to oversee operations and fulfill its supplementary responsibilities to the Church.
